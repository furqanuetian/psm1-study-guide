## Key Questions & Answers

### Q1: Does the Product Owner tell Developers how to do the work?

**No.** The Developers have **sole discretion** over the "how." No one else tells them how to turn Product Backlog items into Increments of value.

**Why it matters:** This reinforces the Scrum value of **self-management** — the team decides who does what, when, and how.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q2: Must Developers finish one Product Backlog item before starting another?

**No.** The Scrum Guide does not require sequential work. Developers decide the workflow — sequential, parallel, or hybrid — based on context.

**However:** The **Sprint Goal** provides focus. The team should work together toward the Sprint Goal, but the tactics are theirs to decide.

**Why it matters:** Flexibility enables teams to adapt to complexity and emergent requirements.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q3: Can a Scrum Team focus on multiple Product Goals at once?

**No.** The Scrum Team focuses on **one Product Goal at a time**. They must fulfill or abandon one objective before taking on the next.

**But:** They still work on **multiple Product Backlog items** within each Sprint to move toward that single goal.

**Why it matters:** Focus reduces complexity and provides a clear long-term target.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q4: Who creates the Sprint Backlog?

**The Developers are accountable** for the Sprint Backlog. However, it is created through **collaboration of the entire Scrum Team** during Sprint Planning.

**Components:**
- **Sprint Goal (Why):** Entire Scrum Team collaborates
- **Selected PBIs (What):** Developers + Product Owner
- **Actionable Plan (How):** Developers' sole discretion

**Why it matters:** The Sprint Backlog is a plan **by and for the Developers** — they own it and adapt it daily.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q5: Who is accountable for the Sprint Backlog?

**The Developers.** They are accountable for creating, updating, and adapting the Sprint Backlog throughout the Sprint.

**Key details:**
- They update it daily as they learn more
- The Sprint Goal is their commitment
- No one outside tells them how to do the work

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020
