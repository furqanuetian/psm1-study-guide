# Day 3 — Scrum Guide 2020: Advanced Q&A

**Activity:** Second full review of Scrum Guide 2020. Asked NotebookLM advanced, scenario-based questions to clarify Increment delivery, Definition of Done, Product Backlog delegation, Sprint scope negotiation, and multi-team alignment.

---

## Key Questions & Answers

### Q1: Can an Increment be delivered to stakeholders before the Sprint Review?

**Yes.** Multiple Increments may be created within a single Sprint, and any of them may be delivered to stakeholders prior to the Sprint Review.

**Key details:**
- An Increment is "born" the moment a Product Backlog item meets the **Definition of Done**
- If it does *not* meet the Definition of Done, it **cannot be released** or presented
- The Sprint Review is **not a gate** — it's an inspection opportunity

**Why it matters:** Value can flow continuously. The Sprint Review is about inspection and adaptation, not approval.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q2: Does the Sprint Review act as a gate for releasing value?

**No.** The Guide explicitly states that the "Sprint Review should never be considered a gate to releasing value."

**Purpose of the Review:**
- Inspect the outcome of the Sprint
- Adapt the Product Backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders
- Determine next steps

**Why it matters:** Releasing value is continuous — not tied to a ceremony.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q3: If the Definition of Done is not an organizational standard, who creates it?

**The Scrum Team** must create a Definition of Done appropriate for the product.

**Key details:**
- If multiple Scrum Teams work on the same product, they must **mutually define** and comply with the same Definition of Done
- The goal is to ensure transparency and a **shared understanding** of what "done" means

**Why it matters:** Quality is non-negotiable — the team defines it, but they must be aligned.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q4: Can the Product Owner delegate Product Backlog management?

**Yes.** The Product Owner can delegate the work, but **remains accountable** for the results.

**Key details:**
- Creation and communication of backlog items can be delegated
- The Product Owner's decisions (ordering, content) must be respected by the organization

**Why it matters:** Delegation ≠ abdication. Accountability stays with the Product Owner.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q5: Can the Sprint Backlog scope be renegotiated during the Sprint?

**Yes.** The scope of the Sprint Backlog may be clarified and renegotiated with the Product Owner as more is learned.

**However:**
- No changes that would **endanger the Sprint Goal**
- Quality **does not decrease**

**Why it matters:** Flexibility within boundaries — the Sprint Goal is fixed, but scope is negotiable.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

---

### Q6: When multiple Scrum Teams work on the same product, what must they share?

**They must share:**
1. **Same Product Goal**
2. **Same Product Backlog**
3. **Same Product Owner**
4. **Same Definition of Done** (must mutually define and comply)

**Why it matters:** Alignment across teams ensures a single, cohesive product vision.

**Source:** Scrum Guide 2020

| Sprint Review | Not a gate — it's for inspection and adaptation |

## What I Learned Today

- The Sprint Review is **not** a release gate — it's for inspection
- Value can flow continuously — no need to wait for the Review
- The Definition of Done is a team responsibility, not an organizational mandate (unless multiple teams)
- The Product Owner can delegate, but accountability stays with them
- Sprint scope is negotiable — but the Sprint Goal and quality are fixed
- Multiple teams on one product must be aligned on 4 key elements: Goal, Backlog, Owner, and Definition of Done
